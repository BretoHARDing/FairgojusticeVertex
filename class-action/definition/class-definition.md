# CLASS ACTION DEFINITION
## Fair Go Justice v. [Defendants]

### PROPOSED CLASS DEFINITION

**Class Period:** 2015 - Present (Ongoing)

**Geographic Scope:** Australia (subject to legal review, initially focused on NSW)

**Class Members:** All persons who were employed by or contracted with [Defendants] as drivers or mechanics, or who were policy holders with [Insurance Company Defendant], and who:
1. Were assigned to operate or service vehicles that were not maintained in a safe, roadworthy condition.
2. Suffered financial loss, reputational damage, or emotional distress as a result of mechanical failures, accidents, or subsequent investigations.
3. Were subjected to blame-shifting, document backdating, or evidence tampering by [Defendants] following a vehicle incident.

### COMMONALITY FACTORS

#### Shared Conduct:
- Systematic failure to maintain commercial vehicles.
- Fraudulent VIN tampering on written-off or damaged vehicles ("rebirthing").
- Backdating of documents to create a false record of compliance or to shift blame.
- Coordinated practice of blaming drivers for incidents caused by mechanical failure.
- Deliberate suppression or alteration of exculpatory evidence.

#### Shared Harm:
- Unjust deduction of wages.
- Exposure to legal threats and intimidation.
- Wrongful termination or being forced out of work.
- Reputational damage within the industry.
- Financial loss due to accidents and subsequent disputes.
- Emotional distress and psychological harm.

#### Common Questions of Law/Fact:
- Did [Defendants] have a duty of care to ensure vehicle safety?
- Did [Defendants] engage in a systematic practice of VIN rebirthing and document fraud?
- Does the pattern of conduct constitute a breach of contract, negligence, or fraud?
- To what extent are class members entitled to damages for lost income, reputational harm, and other losses?

### SUB-CLASSES (if applicable):
- **Driver Sub-Class:** Those who operated faulty vehicles and were directly blamed for incidents.
- **Mechanic Sub-Class:** Those who were pressured to sign off on unsafe vehicles or ignore defects.
- **Policy Holder Sub-Class:** Those whose insurance claims may have been affected by the VIN fraud.
